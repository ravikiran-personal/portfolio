About

The unusual path
that made me useful.

I started in mechanical engineering — Masters from HAN University, Netherlands, specialising in vehicle dynamics and autonomous systems. Then I pivoted hard. Not because I failed at engineering, but because I found something more interesting: the mechanics of how businesses grow.

At Zomato, I moved from product manager to CEO of Tamil Nadu in under two years — owning a ₹100Cr+ P&L, 12 cities, 50+ people, and end-to-end supply, ops, logistics, and product. Each step was earned by combining data instinct with operational speed.

At Shree Anandhaas (backed by A91 Partners), I took a beloved traditional brand and doubled its digital contribution in 3 months — building the e-commerce stack, launching RFID quick-checkout, and defining the brand language from scratch.

What makes me different isn't breadth for its own sake. It's the ability to move fluidly between product thinking, commercial instinct, and people leadership — and make them work as one system, not three silos. I'm drawn to fast-moving businesses that need someone who can think in systems and execute in sprints.
